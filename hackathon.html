This project matters because currently Docs is geared towards experienced IT professionals and are not user-friendly to university students. Microsoft wants to ensure university students are able to navigate the Docs platform and use it successfully. Our sponsors want to create brand loyalty for the use of their programs.

Information problem: The Docs platform has been developed to a high level of functionality, but is currently only usable by senior developers and IT professionals with years of experience. Students require access to information customized to their experience levels in order to understand how to use this platform.

Problem statement: How might Microsoft tailor the Microsoft Docs platform so they can engage with university students in the US?

Our final deliverable: Microsoft Encounter

Abstract: Microsoft Learn was designed to be a student-facing platform to help develop technical skills but the experience was targeted towards professionals. Our team collaborated with university students to re-architect the site and build a platform that was more enticing to their skill level. By customizing the platform to better fit their needs, our solution, Microsoft Encounter, alleviates the major pain points of the Learn platform and delivers all the key features students look for in a technical learning site. In turn, this will lead to brand loyalty and turn the students of today into their customers of tomorrow.
